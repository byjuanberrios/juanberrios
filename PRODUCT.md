# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers of Juan's writing. People who arrive at the site for the writing itself — essays, notes, bookmarks, playlists — and stay because the reading experience feels good. They are not here as clients or recruits first; they are here as readers. The job to be done is quiet, sustained reading: landing on a post, flowing through an archive, wandering into bookmarks or playlists without friction or noise. The site should feel like settling into a good book — generous whitespace, unhurried pace, nothing competing with the prose.

## Product Purpose

A personal corner of the web where Juan shares thoughts, tastes, and digital experiments. Expression first: the site is the work, not a funnel to a product. It exists to give the writing a good home and to let the craft of designing and building it be quietly visible. Success is a reader who lingers, returns, and trusts that the next post will be worth their time.

## Brand Personality

Minimal and quiet. Personal and hand-made. Editorial and literary. Crafted and precise. The voice is unselfconscious — a real person's space, not a portfolio pitch. Warmth is carried by the writing, the typography, and small hand-crafted details, never by ornament that hasn't earned its place. The designer-developer craft is felt in the type pairing, the rhythm, and the small things rather than announced.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS landing-page tropes.** Hero-metric stat blocks, gradient accents, saturated banner sections, "big number + small label" templates, identical icon-card grids. This is a personal blog, not a product launch.
- **Brutalist / maximalist type.** Oversized display that sacrifices readability for impact, type experiments that turn the page into noise, brutalist grids that fight the prose. The writing is the point; the type serves it.
- The warm beige / cream / sand / paper default is already avoided here (the site uses stone/zinc neutrals with lime accents) — keep refusing it. "Warmth" comes from the voice and the type, not a tinted near-white body background.

## Design Principles

1. **Reading first.** Reading comfort governs every decision — measure (65–75ch), leading, contrast, pace. Decoration is permitted only when it doesn't compete with the prose. A post page that's slightly quieter is always better than one that's slightly louder.
2. **Practice what you preach.** The site is itself the proof of craft. Precision is felt in the typography, the spacing rhythm, and the small details — not announced with "look at this" flourishes. A frontend designer-developer's personal site that doesn't feel hand-built undercuts everything it says.
3. **Personal, not promotional.** This is a real person's corner of the web. Voice over pitch. The writing, the bookmarks, the playlists are the substance; the design gets out of their way and lets a person come through.
4. **Quiet by default.** Restraint is the baseline. Every ornament must earn its place; whitespace is a feature, not wasted space. When in doubt, take it out.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 against its background; large text ≥ 3:1. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation with a crossfade or instant fallback. Full keyboard operability for the menu, theme toggle, and any interactive element. Spanish (Chilean) content; dates and language attributes already handled as `es-CL` / `lang="es"`.
